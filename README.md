# Visual Protocol for Cellular Immunofluorescence (IF) Staining

## 1. Project Overview
This repository contains a high-resolution, conference-ready visual protocol for **Cellular Immunofluorescence (IF) Staining**. The project was developed for a **SciArt Biotechnology** assignment to demonstrate the synthesis of peer-reviewed laboratory methods into a clear, instructional infographic suitable for scientific posters and biotechnology documentation.

## 2. Scientific Basis & Citation
The steps outlined in the visual protocol and this README are derived from the standardized methodologies for indirect immunofluorescence in cultured cells as detailed in:

> **Reference:** Im, K., Mareninov, S., Diaz, M. F., & Yong, W. H. (2019). *An Introduction to Performing Immunofluorescence Staining*. Methods in Molecular Biology, vol 1897. Humana Press, New York, NY.
> **DOI:** [10.1007/978-1-4939-8935-5_26](https://doi.org/10.1007/978-1-4939-8935-5_26)

## 3. Detailed Experimental Protocol

### Phase I: Sample Preparation & Fixation
1.  **Cell Seeding:** Seed target cells on sterile glass coverslips or chamber slides. Allow cells to reach 60–80% confluency to ensure individual cell morphology is visible.
2.  **Fixation:** Aspirate growth media and wash with PBS. Incubate cells in **4% Paraformaldehyde (PFA)** in PBS for 15 minutes at room temperature.
    * *Mechanism:* PFA creates covalent cross-links between molecules, "freezing" the cellular structure in place.

### Phase II: Permeabilization & Blocking
3.  **Permeabilization:** Incubate cells in **0.1% to 0.5% Triton X-100** in PBS for 10 minutes.
    * *Mechanism:* This detergent creates microscopic pores in the lipid bilayer, allowing large antibody molecules to access intracellular targets.
4.  **Blocking:** Incubate in **5% Bovine Serum Albumin (BSA)** or Normal Goat Serum in PBS for 30–60 minutes.
    * *Mechanism:* BSA occupies "sticky" non-specific binding sites, reducing background noise (auto-fluorescence).

### Phase III: Antibody Incubation
5.  **Primary Antibody:** Dilute the primary antibody (specific to the protein of interest) in blocking buffer (e.g., 1:200). Incubate overnight at 4°C in a humidified chamber.
6.  **Secondary Antibody & Nuclear Counterstain:** Wash with PBS-T (0.1% Tween-20). Incubate with **fluorophore-conjugated secondary antibodies** (e.g., 1:500) and **DAPI** (for DNA staining) for 1 hour at room temperature in the dark.

### Phase IV: Mounting & Imaging
7.  **Final Washes:** Wash 3x for 5 minutes each with PBS-T to remove unbound antibodies.
8.  **Mounting:** Apply a drop of anti-fade mounting medium onto a glass slide. Carefully invert the coverslip onto the drop.
9.  **Visualization:** Image using a Confocal or Widefield Fluorescence Microscope using appropriate excitation/emission filters.

## 4. Design Methodology (SciArt Synthesis)
To transform the Im et al. (2019) text into the provided infographic, the following design principles were applied:

* **Information Hierarchy:** Steps were categorized into four distinct "Action Zones" (Sample Prep, Chemistry, Immunology, Imaging) to improve the flow of information.
* **Visual Symbols:** Standardized iconography was used for reagents (beakers/droppers) and molecular structures (Y-shaped IgG antibodies).
* **Color Palette:** A high-contrast palette was selected to mimic the dark-room environment of fluorescence microscopy. 
* **Critical Alerts:** "CRITICAL" warnings (e.g., "Avoid Drying!") were placed at steps where dehydration significantly impacts protein antigenicity.
